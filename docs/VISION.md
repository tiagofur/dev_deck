# DevDeck.ai Vision

> **Your AI-assisted external memory for development work.**

[Leer en español](VISION.es.md)

---

## 1. The Problem
Developers discover a massive amount of information daily: useful repos, niche CLIs, IDE plugins, macOS shortcuts, complex snippets, AI prompts, and workflows.

**The current solution is broken:**
- **Browser Bookmarks:** A "black hole" where links go to die. No metadata, no context.
- **Stars (GitHub):** Only for repos. Hard to search inside a list of 1000+ stars.
- **Notes (Notion/Obsidian):** Too much friction for a quick save. Requires manual organization.
- **Slack/Discord/X:** Useful links get lost in the feed after 5 minutes.

**Real world scenario:** *"I remember I saw a CLI to resize images in a thread last month, but I don't remember its name, and searching 'image resize cli' gives me 100 generic results."*

---

## 2. The Solution: DevDeck.ai
DevDeck is a **high-signal, low-friction** vault for dev knowledge.

### 2.1 Core Pillars
1. **Offline-First & Native:** It must be fast. Instant search, instant capture.
2. **AI-Enriched:** You don't tag; the AI tags for you. It summarizes READMEs, extracts installation commands, and understands the *intent* of the tool.
3. **Multi-Channel Capture:** Save from the terminal, browser, or clipboard.
4. **Social & Collaborative:** Discover what other developers are using.

---

## 3. Differentiators
- **Semantic Search:** Don't search for "ffmpeg". Search for "convert video to gif". The AI knows which tool does that.
- **Native Experience:** A desktop app (Electron) that feels like part of the OS (shortcuts, global search).
- **Zero Friction:** "Copy URL -> Open DevDeck -> Saved". No folders to choose, no tags to type.
- **Open Source:** Your knowledge vault shouldn't be a black box.

---

## 4. Target Audience
- **The "Curious Dev":** Always exploring new tools.
- **The "Efficiency Freak":** Needs the right command at their fingertips.
- **Open Source Maintainers:** To promote their niche tools to a targeted audience.

---

## 5. Long-term Vision
DevDeck aims to become the **standard repository for developer workflows**. Not just a place to store links, but a place to store *how you work*.

*"DevDeck is to your knowledge what Git is to your code."*
